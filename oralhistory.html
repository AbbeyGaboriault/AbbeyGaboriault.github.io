Samuel Proctor Oral History Program at the University of Florida (SPOHP)

SPOHP at UF is "the official oral history program of the University of Florida, featuring over 5,000 interviews and more than 150,000 pages of transcribed material in the SPOHP archives and Digital Collections at the University of Florida. Our primary mission is to gather, preserve, and promote living histories of individuals from all walks of life."

I am a member of this organization and regularly work with them to transcribe past interviews and interview individuals myself for our collection. One of the highlights of my experience was the trip we took this past fall. Every year, SPOHP goes to the Mississippi Delta to research the civil rights movement with veterans and activists from that time. Participants are able to meet and work with many individuals as well as visit sites where history was made.

The following are interviews with Diana Dombrowski, undergraduate coordinator and site director; Sarah Blanc, senior research development coordinator; and Paul Ortiz, program director of SPOHP. These interviews detail the research trip to the Mississippi Delta.
